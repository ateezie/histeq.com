FAQs

Quick answers to common questions about historic tax credit investments

Historic tax credits are tax incentives available that provide benefits for owners who rehabilitate a certified historic structure and encourage preservation of historic properties.

To qualify, your property must be listed on the National Register of Historic Places or deemed to contribute to a National Register Historic District. The rehabilitation must meet specific standards.

We focus on commercial and residential historic rehabilitation projects across various sectors including hospitality, residential and commercial real estate.

Historic tax credits can provide up to 20% federal tax credit and additional state credits, significantly improving investment returns and financing capabilities for qualifying projects.

The process typically takes 6-18 months, depending on project complexity, property condition, and approval time from historic preservation authorities.
